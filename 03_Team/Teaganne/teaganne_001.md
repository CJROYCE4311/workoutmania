Teaganne Finn is an accomplished CrossFit Level 2 (CF-L2) trainer and journalist who currently coaches at CrossFit Union Square in New York City. She has successfully transitioned from a career in political journalism to becoming a leading voice and coach within the CrossFit community.


Coaching & Professional Roles
CrossFit Level 2 Trainer: She holds a CF-L2 certification and is a coach at CrossFit Union Square (Manhattan) and CrossFit 201 (New Jersey).

Journalism: She is a Senior Writer for Morning Chalk Up, a major news outlet for the CrossFit community. She previously worked as a journalist covering breaking news and politics in Washington, D.C., for organizations like Bloomberg.


Experience: Before moving to New York, she spent five years in Washington, D.C., where she began her coaching career at CrossFit Balance.

Athletic Background & Achievements
Teaganne has a diverse athletic history and remains a competitive athlete in the CrossFit circuit.

Current Competition: * 2025 Syndicate Crown: Competed in the Team division.

2024 & 2025 Wodapalooza: Competed in the Team division.

2023 Asbury Park Summer Games: Team competitor.

Multi-Sport Background: She grew up playing lacrosse, swimming, and tennis. In college, she was a walk-on for the rowing team and later completed several sprint triathlons and a half-Ironman.


CrossFit Rankings: In the 2025 CrossFit Open, she ranked 2,012th worldwide in the Women's division and 571st in the North America East region.

Education
Master of Arts (MA): Journalism, American University.

Bachelor of Science (BS): Hobart and William Smith Colleges.

Coaching Philosophy
Teaganne is known for her belief in the CrossFit class dynamic and the power of community. Her approach is built on the standard of "treating others the way you want to be treated," and she is a strong advocate for athletes of all levels, focusing on building a positive and inclusive gym environment.
